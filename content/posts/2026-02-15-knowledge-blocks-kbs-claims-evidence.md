# Unleashing the Power of Knowledge Blocks (KBs): Claims + Evidence

In the world of Knowledge-Based Currency (KBC), Knowledge Blocks (KBs) play a crucial role in transforming verifiable knowledge into a secure digital asset. KBs are the building blocks of the decentralized knowledge economy, where claims are backed by evidence, creating a transparent and trustworthy ecosystem. Let's dive deep into the concept of KBs, exploring how they are shaping the future of information exchange and why they matter in the grand scheme of KBC.

## Overview

**Key Principles of Knowledge Blocks (KBs):**
1. **Claims:** Knowledge claims are assertions of truth or facts made by contributors within the KBC ecosystem. These claims can range from scientific discoveries to historical events, all of which are stored on the distributed ledger known as K-Chain.
2. **Evidence:** Unlike traditional information systems, KBs require evidence to support knowledge claims. This evidence can come in various forms, such as research papers, data sets, or expert opinions, ensuring the credibility and reliability of the information.
3. **K-Chain:** The distributed ledger where knowledge claims and evidence are stored immutably, allowing for transparency and traceability in the knowledge economy.
4. **LightWeb:** A decentralized network that facilitates the efficient sharing of information across the KBC ecosystem, enabling seamless access to a vast array of knowledge resources.
5. **Oracle AI:** The intelligent engine that validates and retrieves knowledge on demand, ensuring the accuracy and relevance of the information exchanged within the KBC platform.
6. **Proof-of-Knowledge:** Instead of traditional proofs of work or stake, KBC rewards contributors for their expertise and contributions to the knowledge economy through Proof-of-Knowledge, incentivizing a collaborative and inclusive community.
