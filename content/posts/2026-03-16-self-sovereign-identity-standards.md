# The Power of Self-Sovereign Identity Standards in the KBC Ecosystem

In today's digital age, the concept of self-sovereign identity has become increasingly important. Self-sovereign identity standards give individuals control over their own personal data and enable them to securely manage and share their identity information online. This matters greatly to the Knowledge-Based Currency (KBC) movement because it aligns with the core values of empowerment, security, and trust that KBC aims to uphold.

## Overview

Self-sovereign identity standards are based on the principles of autonomy, privacy, and security. In the context of KBC, these standards are crucial for ensuring that individuals have ownership and control over their knowledge claims and expertise. Here's how the key components of the KBC ecosystem relate to self-sovereign identity standards:

- **K-Chain**: The distributed ledger that stores immutable knowledge claims. Self-sovereign identity standards ensure that individuals have the authority to manage and update their own knowledge claims on the K-Chain.
  
- **LightWeb**: The decentralized network for sharing information efficiently. Self-sovereign identity standards protect users' privacy and enable them to selectively share their knowledge with others on the LightWeb.
  
- **Oracle AI**: The intelligent engine that validates and retrieves knowledge on demand. Self-sovereign identity standards ensure that individuals can trust the information retrieved by Oracle AI as it is sourced from verifiable experts.
  
- **Proof-of-Knowledge**: The reward system for contributors based on their expertise. Self-sovereign identity standards validate the credentials and expertise of contributors, ensuring a fair and trustworthy knowledge economy.
